What is Aquaponics?

Aquaponics is an innovative, closed-loop system that brilliantly combines aquaculture (fish farming) and hydroponics (soilless plant cultivation).

It's a symbiotic dance where fish waste nourishes plants, and plants naturally filter the water for the fish. The result? A highly efficient, sustainable, and organic method of food production.

How AquaGrow Works: The Symbiotic Cycle

1. Fish Excretion

Fish produce ammonia-rich waste in their tanks.

2. Bacterial Conversion

Beneficial bacteria convert ammonia into nitrates, a perfect plant food.

3. Plant Absorption

Plants absorb these nitrates, cleaning the water for the fish.

4. Water Recirculation

Clean water circulates back to the fish tanks, completing the cycle.

This elegant closed-loop system ensures optimal health for both fish and plants, naturally and efficiently.

The Intelligence Behind AquaGrow: Our Sensors

Sensors are the "eyes and ears" of your system, ensuring a balanced ecosystem.

pH Sensors

Continuously monitor water acidity/alkalinity for optimal nutrient absorption and fish health.

Temperature Sensors

Track water and ambient temperatures, crucial for fish metabolism and plant growth.

Water Level Sensors

Ensure consistent water levels, preventing pump damage and maintaining system integrity.

Humidity Sensors

Monitor air humidity around plants to prevent issues like mold and ensure healthy growth.

Powered by Arduino, these sensors convert physical conditions into electrical signals, which our system processes to maintain ideal conditions.
